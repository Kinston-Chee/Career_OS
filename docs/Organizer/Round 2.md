## 05 — The Build

# The 28-Day Build

Map your build early — the sooner you plan, the smoother the 28 days run.

### What To Make Sure Happens

- **Clear architecture and scope** — know what you're building, and what you're not.
- **Lean on AI** — it's an AI-native hackathon. Use it to build, design, debug, and write.
- **A working marketplace** — at least one core module wired up and running end-to-end.
- **Your differentiators** — the modules that make your build stand out. Show range.
- **A live demo** — deployed and clickable, not a video.
- **Documentation** — enough for someone outside your team to follow.
- **Talk to your mentor** — especially when you're stuck on direction or scope.

### A Note Before You Start

Four weeks is short, and the instinct is to start coding.

Don't — spend the first days on architecture.

The builds that stand out are the ones our engineers can build on.

## 06 — The Build

# Common Pitfalls

Lessons from proposal reviews and earlier-cohort patterns.

---

### Pitfall 01

## Ignoring the Strategic Context

Building "an AI thing for careers" instead of a specific Career OS problem.

Product & UX thinking carries the most weight, at **30%**.

---

### Pitfall 02

## Breadth With No Depth

Range is good, but five half-working modules impress no one.

Get one core flow running end-to-end before you spread out.

---

### Pitfall 03

## Leaving Integration To The End

Wiring everything together in the final days is where builds break.

Deploy early and integrate as you go, so the demo runs on the day.
